Pothos (Epipremnum aureum)

Pothos is one of the easiest houseplants to grow and is a beautiful addition to any home or office.

Best of all, pothos plants grow well in almost any situation! Native to Mo'orea, an island in French Polynesia,

pothos is a trailing vine with glossy or satiny, heart-shaped leaves.

Pothos Care

Pothos plants like to dry out quite a bit between waterings. If you notice leaves yellowing or falling off, you may have waited too long.

Dry, brown leaf edges also mean water more frequently.

Be sure to drench the plant thoroughly each time you water (until water runs out of the drainage holes in the pot) and then let it dry before watering again.

Make sure to water you pothos every 1-2 week and make sure to let the soil dry out between watering

Pothos are toxic to both dogs and cats

Pothos also help purify the air and they remove the folloing toxins:formaldehyde, xylene, and benzene.
